Advocacy Services

We help Eritrean parents navigate the educational system and advocate effectively for their children's educational needs and rights.

Our Advocacy Approach

We believe that parents are the most important advocates for their children. Our approach focuses on empowering Eritrean parents with the knowledge, skills, and support they need to effectively advocate for their children within the educational system.

We understand the unique cultural perspectives and challenges that Eritrean families face when navigating a new educational system. Our advocacy services are designed to bridge cultural gaps and ensure that children receive the educational support they deserve.

Our Advocacy Services

One-on-One Advocacy Support

Our trained advocates provide personalized support to parents facing specific challenges with their child's education. We can help with:

  • Understanding your child's rights in the educational system
  • Requesting and interpreting educational assessments
  • Developing strategies for effective communication with educators
  • Attending school meetings with parents when needed
  • Ensuring appropriate program placements and support services

Parent Education Workshops

We offer workshops designed to equip parents with advocacy skills and knowledge:

  • Understanding the educational system structure
  • Navigating special education programs and services
  • Recognizing signs that your child may need additional support
  • Strategies for supporting learning at home
  • Cultural considerations in educational advocacy

School Council Participation

We encourage and support Eritrean parents in becoming active members of school councils:

  • Information about the role and importance of school councils
  • Guidance on how to join and participate effectively
  • Support for overcoming language or cultural barriers to participation
  • Networking with other parent advocates

Educational Assessment Support

We help parents navigate the assessment process to ensure their children's needs are properly identified:

  • Understanding when and how to request educational assessments
  • Explaining different types of assessments and their purposes
  • Interpreting assessment results and recommendations
  • Following up to ensure recommended supports are implemented

Our Advocacy Process

Initial Consultation

We begin with a thorough consultation to understand your child's specific situation, your concerns, and your goals. This helps us develop a personalized advocacy plan.

Education and Empowerment

We provide you with information about your rights, the educational system, and specific strategies relevant to your situation. Our goal is to empower you as your child's primary advocate.

Action Planning

Together, we develop a concrete action plan with specific steps, timelines, and goals. This may include requesting assessments, arranging meetings with educators, or seeking program changes.

Implementation Support

We provide ongoing support as you implement the action plan, including preparation for meetings, review of documents, and guidance on effective communication strategies.

Follow-up and Adjustment

We check in regularly to monitor progress, celebrate successes, and adjust strategies as needed. Our support continues until your advocacy goals are achieved.

Community Connection

We connect you with other Eritrean parents who have faced similar challenges, creating a supportive community network for sharing experiences and strategies.
